About

Odumeleng Ramatlo is Executive Director of The Now School of Thought and Founder of the virtual school. With 11 years of working experience in academic administration management in the higher education environment, and experience as a Communications Lecturer speaks of his commitment to, and passion for education. His experience of working at the University of Johannesburg, University of the Witwatersrand and Henley Business School gives Odumeleng the benefit of referencing externally for best ways of achieving meaningful learning and engagement methodologies, as well as efficient academic administration processes.

“I’m excited to see TNST come to life after months of nurturing this butterfly to finally take to the skies. The staunch commitment of the team affirmed to me that ingenuity cannot survive in pockets…now here we are, and I’m elated for the promise that TNST holds for Africa”.

Odumeleng holds a Postgraduate Diploma in Management Practice (Henley Business School), BA Honours Communication Theory (University of Johannesburg), BA Corporate Communication (University of Johannesburg), Advanced Certificate in Public Governance Cum Laude (University of Johannesburg) and a National Certificate in Public Governance Cum Laude also from UJ’s Department of Public Management and Governance. Mr Ramatlo is currently an enrolled candidate for a Masters in Management degree with Wits Business School and with results pending the outcome of a dissertation entitled “Antecedents of Student Experience and Engagement in Selected Business Schools in South Africa”. Odumeleng initiated this project while employed at Henley Business School as Senior Admissions Manager

“Education is not an automatic process. There are facilitating conditions for education to be effective especially if it is going to inspire desirable school-leaver attributes such as adaptability and resourcefulness. We’re confident TNST’s curriculum is instrumental in harnessing such attributes and more”.

Odumeleng is confident that investing in young minds through shared platforms of knowledge testing and generation is the answer to designing a desirable future that has not come into existence. This is a future which learners should be prepared for if they are to improve the economy and social climate of Africa.
