Giving to TNST

The Now School of Thought (TNST) operates on the generous financial contributions from funders. Through your giving to TNST you would be our change partner in designing learning experiences for achieving desirable school-leaver attributes such as critical thinking, personal mastery and thought leadership. The future of South Africa, in Africa, is in all our hands. You can also be involved in sculpting future-fit leaders for the realities of the roles they have to play in society, the industrial revolution and Africa Rising. For more information on larger scale funding please email Vuyo Wakaba (vuyow@tnst.org.za)

Partner with TNST

TNST would not have the ability to evolve and fully use the opportunity of touching one delegate at a time without the direct and indirect result of our partnerships and collaboration with businesses, individuals and other organisations in our eco-system. Through partnerships we are able to deliver our programmes at no cost to the learners (our delegates). As conduits for the successful delivery of our programmes, TNST believes in cultivating robust partnerships which are inspired by trust and confidence in all their potential. Our networked collaboration through financial giving, mentoring, delivery partnerships, board-serving and volunteering is a value chain in which every role player is key in preparing learners today for active participation in the economy, and a frame of reference for assuming positions of positive impact in society through a heightened awareness of self. To partner with TNST please email Tamara Moss (tamaram@tnst.org.za)

Mentor at TNST

Stories told above stories lived are compelling enough to inspire change or set one on a path of pursuing their truth. Mentorship by industry players, social activists and leaders has a place at TNST to guide our delegates in designing their alternative or desired futures. To be a mentor at TNST and make a difference in the lives of our delegates the opportunity is there for you. The terms of mentorship are customised to the mentor’s availability and programme delivery needs. If you’re interested in becoming a mentor on our programmes please contact Nokwazi Maseko (nokwazim@tnst.org.za)

Facilitate at TNST

Facilitators are important for the successful delivery of TNST programmes. Facilitators are industry players or individuals who have a record of social activism for inclusivity, with at least an undergraduate degree. Facilitators with an interest in the Humanities and critical thinking are key at TNST. If you are interested in becoming a TNST facilitator, please contact Nokwazi Maseko (nokwazim@tnst.org.za)
