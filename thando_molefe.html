About

Thando Molefe takes the lead for TNST’s curriculum planning and development. Thando’s passion for human rights and social justice (especially for marginalised youth and communities) has steered him into the Education field of work.

“As an educator in the humanities, I am actively engaged and involved with the intricate ‘calling’ to transform and develop the critical cognitive layers of our future leaders- a duty I assume with honour”.

Mr Molefe believes that The Now School of Thought programmes provide platforms which will transform and challenge the way TNST delegates view themselves in relation to global issues which not only affect their daily lives, but who they are and who they want to become.

“I thrive on critical thinking and my will to disrupt social norms which suffocate individual thinking and discourage collaborative engagement. At TNST I work with like-minded people on a journey of pursuing social truths for both its pain and pleasure in order to weave a truly democratic perspective”.

Thando’s democratic perspective is one that is equitable in resource access, equal employment opportunities and one that exposes injustices in all its facets to (re)create an inclusive system of governance and educational deliveries which serve all its people, equally.
