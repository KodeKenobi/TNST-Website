Message from the Executive Director

In South Africa, the voices of struggling youth can be heard over battles because their socio-economic environment has not afforded them sufficient tools to match post-school realities relating to integration in higher education and the workforce. This pre-entry deficit is attributed to poor learner socialisation, and TNST’s answer is personal mastery, critical thinking and thought leadership at Senior FET phase in order to inspire desirable school-leaver attributes such as resourcefulness and adaptability. We believe that our mission in pioneering a supplementary educational project of critical thinking, and a place of discovery for personal mastery constructs aligns with the phenomenon of Africa Rising where young South Africans should be prepared to harness all the opportunities available to them in the wake of the 4th Industrial Revolution and beyond. The solution that TNST has is not for a single race or a single class, however it is really two things. It’s a social fit and it’s an African solution.

Odumeleng Ramatlo

CEO, Founder, Executive Director and Facilitator

Odumeleng Ramatlo is Executive Director of The Now School of Thought and Founder of the virtual school. With 11 years of working experience in academic administration management in the higher education environment, and experience as a Communications Lecturer speaks of his commitment to, and passion for education. His experience of working at the University of Johannesburg, University of the Witwatersrand, Henley Business School and Cape Peninsula University of Technology gives Odumeleng the benefit of referencing externally for best ways of achieving meaningful learning and engagement methodologies, as well as efficient academic administration processes. Odumeleng is currently Faculty Manager for the Faculty of Education at Cape Peninsula University of Technology.

Who we are

TNST is founded on the growing need to equip school leavers with critical thinking and personal mastery

TNST is founded on the growing need to equip school leavers with critical thinking and personal mastery tools which are necessary for them to effectively function with a heightened awareness of self and the realities of the world they live in. As they’re being brought into the mainstream of the economy, TNST delegates will be well poised to assume a position of positive impact in society as our commitment to Africa Rising. TNST will achieve this simply by creating advantage for our delegates with a platform where they can help themselves, each other and create meaningful connections as they adapt for the future and the promise it holds. Our approach democratises the consumption experience of education and recognises that our delegates have a mind which is an extension of just the brain, and our facilitators as industry players will connect experiences with the unique curriculum using a socratic method of delivery modelled on dialogue and interaction. Our programme delivery is rooted in independent judgment, enquiry and thinking. TNST will partner with its delegates and beneficiaries and will bear the torch in exploring the multiple stories of the world that we live in by acknowledging that our worldviews are not untrue, but rather an incomplete reality. TNST will strive for all its ideals and give value to the Humanities, soft skills and the human mind in the emergence of Africa Rising and the rise of the 4th industrial revolution and beyond.

Our Vision

The Now School of Thought aspires to be the pioneering and leading institution in alternative and supplementary education that equips delegates with soft skills that are relevant for the Fourth Industrial Revolution. Delegate outcomes of critical thinking and personal mastery at the basic education level will firmly embed TNST’s social citizenship by 2026.

Our Mission

The Mission of The Now School of Thought (TNST) recognises the promise that exists within the youth of Africa and strives to aid them in the quest to realise their potential by serving as the pioneering alternative and supplementary educational project of critical thinking, and to be a place of discovery for personal mastery constructs. TNST will achieve this by building on the principles of intellectual stimulation, market leadership and local relevance. As a leading African institution that is attuned to the skills needed for young people to succeed in the Fourth Industrial Revolution, TNST is built on principles of stimulating critical thought and sense making. We are committed to providing quality platforms of knowledge testing, necessary alternative education and an evolving curriculum, founded on an ever-changing world, cutting edge research and facilitation, public engagement and productive partnerships with brand-aligned institutions that speak to TNST’s social purpose, and which provide corporate social opportunity in achieving its mission.
