About

Nokwazi Maseko is Director for Governance and Programme Delivery at TNST and spearheads the successful delivery of programmes in line with acceptable operating procedures. Ms Maseko not only believes in providing on-key support to our delegates through partnership, but also believes in providing our delegates with a holistic student experience by managing delegate expectations and involvement with the social and affective engagement strategies required to inform positive delegate wellbeing. Programme delivery is a critical portfolio at TNST as curriculum planning and development, and programme management reside in her portfolio. Nokwazi is passionate about social justice and critical thinking. Ms Maseko has been employed in the higher education industry working with undergraduate students in the Registrar’s Division, currently a Senior Faculty Officer for undergraduate studies at the University of the Witwatersrand. Curriculum planning and student counselling significantly shape her role, alongside student progression and academic administration management.

“These varied roles have opened my mind to issues of social justice and have highlighted the need to equip more students with critical thinking skills not just for the duration of their studies, but to carry them through all aspects of their lives. This is why I am excited to be part of TNST, an excellent team that seeks to address these issues in Africa.”

Nokwazi has a National Diploma in Public Relations (University of Johannesburg), BA Communication Science (UNISA) and a BA Honours in Communication Theory- Cum Laude (University of Johannesburg). Ms Maseko is currently enrolled for a Masters in Management degree with the Wits School of Governance.

“I believe that TNST will empower people to see beyond what meets the eye, thus creating spaces for innovative solutions to not just social justice issues but to other ills that plague our society today. I also believe that what TNST provides will make a difference in their individual lives and also in the lives of those around them.”
